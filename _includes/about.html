About

Knowledge

As best of my year, I was granted a scholarship by the German National Academic Foundation sponsoring students of outstanding academic achievements. After I finish my Bachelor degree in August 2018, I plan to pursue a Master's in IT Systems Engineering with focus on digital health.

Experience

In order to participate in a CS contest, I taught myself to code when I was 16 years old. Although it was enough for a second prize in the national finals, I like to think that my coding has improved since then. Through wide-ranging employments - e.g. as Research Assistant at the University of Cambridge or as Development Intern at a leading digital health start-up company - I could gather extensive work experience and boost my coding skills. Now I am a proficient Java developer, had a lot of fun with Prolog, fell in love with React.js, briefly met MatLab, Python and some web languages, and still suffer from nightmares about a fromer VHDL/Assembler project.

Passion

I firmly believe that being passionate about something is the real superpower humans can have: it's the root of our potential, the fire of our ideas, and the guide to where we belong. I am lucky to have great passion for the things in my life, because gives me determination to do even what might seem impossible at first - a nice strength especially when debugging code. Also, it gives me the persistence to follow my many extracurricular interests - like medicine, running, spending time with friends and travelling the world - despite studying full-time.
